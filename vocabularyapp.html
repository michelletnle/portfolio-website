Vocabulary App Case Study

Main Focus:

Learn user centric ideology for mobile app creation.

Skills Utilized:

Competitve analysis, user interviews, persona creation, card sorting, wireframing, prototyping, and usability testing.

Introduction

Objective:

To empower people to learn vocabulary or continue with their studies. This project was reviewed through CareerFoundry.

Goal:

To design a mobile application that encourages users to study in any environment.

My Role:

UX Designer

Timeline:

1 month

My Process

  1. User Research
    • Competitive Analysis, User Interviews
  2. User Personas
    • Proto-Persona Creation, User Stories, Problem Statement, Hypothesis
  3. Information Architecture
    • Task Analysis, User Flows, Site-mapping
  4. Wireframing & Prototyping
    • Low Fidelity Wireframing and Prototyping Designs
  5. Usability Testing
    • Conduct Testing, Summarize Findings, Feedback Loop

Overview

Research

The purpose of the app is for people of all backgrounds and ages can learn something new. Where they can use on the go for a quick study session within their busy schedule. To get started, I did a competitive analysis on 3 similar popular apps and how they accomplish their goals. Keeping in mind these following questions:

  • “Why do I think this is a successful product (or not) in terms of meeting the goals of the users?”
  • “What do I think the designers did to make the product successful (or not), in terms of the users?”
  • “How do I feel about using the product? How would I make it better?”

To add to this research, I needed to know firsthand how people feel about using vocabulary apps. I interviewed 3 candidates and summarized the results into what they were doing, thinking, and feeling. From them, Oliver was born.

Design

With enough research materials, I created some user flows to conceptualized the app's pages. Then, created cooresponding low-fidelity wireframes to bring the app to life. Utilizing "Marvel", I conducted five usability test. With their feedback, I revised the app to their concerns.

Challenges

Surprisingly, learning to be user-centric throughout the project was not the hardest part even though that was the main focus of this project. The biggest challenge I faced was conducting the usability testing. Especially, trying to balance telling information without changing the participants' way of thinking and doing.

Main Takeaway

learned from the designs that I need to be more neat and concise. There were times the participant and the app were on the same page but because I did not concisely write it that way, the participant was confused. I also learned the user centric ideology save times in the app creation process. By creating for the user, features are made for them in a way they will understand.
